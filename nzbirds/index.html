Some New
Zealand Birds

Australasian Bittern
matuku
Australasian bitterns make their nests on the ground.
Bellbird
korimako
Bellbirds sing the most in the early morning and late evening.
Black Stilt
kakī
With the spread of new predators and extensive modification of their habitat, by 1981 kakī numbers declined to a low of just 23 birds.
Blue Duck
whio
Blue ducks vigorously defend their river territories all the year round.

Crested Grebe
kāmana
The grebe's legs are set far back, making for excellent diving but poor land mobility.
Fantail
pīwakawaka
Fantails have a pied brown/white/black color scheme, though some purely black faintails are seen.
Grey Warbler
riroriro
Grey warbler was the surprise recipient of the title of New Zealand's best-loved bird in 2007.
Kaka
Kākā
Kākā are acknowledged as one of the most musical of all parrots with a variety of melodic whistles and warbles sitting along more typical parrot-like screeches.
